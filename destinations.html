Explore Our Coastal Destinations

Discover the beauty and serenity of the world's most stunning coastlines.

Santorini, Greece

Santorini is one of the Cyclades islands in the Aegean Sea. It was devastated by a volcanic eruption in the 16th century BC, forever shaping its rugged landscape. The whitewashed, cubiform houses of its 2 principal towns, Fira and Oia, cling to cliffs above an underwater caldera (crater).

Maui, Hawaii

Maui, known also as “The Valley Isle,” is the second largest Hawaiian island. The island is known for its stunning beaches, including the world-famous Ka'anapali Beach, as well as its volcanic landscape and the dormant volcano Haleakala.

Learn More

Amalfi Coast, Italy

The Amalfi Coast is a 50-kilometer stretch of coastline along the southern edge of Italy’s Sorrentine Peninsula, in the Campania region. It's a popular holiday destination, with steep cliffs and a jagged shoreline dotted with small beaches and pastel-colored fishing villages.

Learn More

Bali, Indonesia

Bali, known as the "Island of Gods," is an Indonesian island known for its natural beauty, from the iconic rice paddies to beaches and coral reefs. Offshore, the Nusa islands offer scuba diving and snorkeling sites.

Learn More

Sydney, Australia

Sydney, capital of New South Wales, is one of Australia’s largest cities and is best known for its harbor front Sydney Opera House, with a distinctive sail-like design.

Learn More

Maldives

The Maldives, officially the Republic of Maldives, is a small archipelagic state in South Asia, situated in the Arabian Sea of the Indian Ocean. It lies southwest of Sri Lanka and India.
